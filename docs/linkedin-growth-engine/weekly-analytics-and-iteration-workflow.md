# Weekly analytics and iteration workflow

Use this workflow to review LinkedIn post performance, extract lessons, and turn those lessons into the next planning cycle. The goal is a steady content loop, not a weekly report that disappears into a folder.

## Operating rhythm

Run the review once per week after the most recent posts have had at least 72 hours to gather signal.

1. **Collect metrics before the review.** Export or record performance for every post published during the review window.
2. **Score the week.** Compare each post with the rolling 4-week median, then add qualitative notes from comments, DMs, and profile visits.
3. **Extract lessons.** Translate the data into specific keep, change, stop, and test decisions.
4. **Feed planning.** Update the next content plan before new topics are selected.
5. **Archive the review.** Save the completed review so future planning can trace why a content choice changed.

## Inputs

Capture these inputs before the review starts.

| Input | Source | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Post inventory | LinkedIn profile or content calendar | Include URL, publish date, topic, format, hook, CTA, and target audience. |
| Performance metrics | LinkedIn analytics | Record impressions, reactions, comments, reposts, saves if available, profile visits, follows, link clicks, and CTR if available. |
| Qualitative signal | Comments, DMs, replies, sales conversations | Note repeated objections, language people used, and questions worth answering later. |
| Distribution notes | Manual tracking | Include who engaged early, where the post was shared, and whether collaborators amplified it. |
| Prior plan | Previous weekly review and content backlog | Check whether the week tested the intended hypothesis. |

## Core metrics

Review both reach and business signal. A high-reach post without the right audience may be less useful than a smaller post that starts the right conversations.

| Metric | How to use it |
| --- | --- |
| Impressions | Measures reach. Compare with the rolling 4-week median, not a single outlier week. |
| Engagement rate | Use `(reactions + comments + reposts) / impressions`. Compare posts with similar age and format. |
| Comment quality | Count comments that add a point, ask a question, mention a pain, or signal buying intent. |
| Saves and reposts | Treat these as proof the post was useful enough to keep or share. |
| Profile visits and follows | Use as a proxy for audience fit and trust. |
| Link clicks, DMs, or replies | Use as bottom-of-funnel signal when the CTA asks for action. |

## Review agenda

Timebox the review to 45 minutes.

### 1. Confirm the baseline, 5 minutes

- List the posts reviewed this week.
- Note the rolling 4-week median for impressions and engagement rate.
- Flag posts that have not had 72 hours of runtime. Review them lightly and revisit next week.

### 2. Identify winners and misses, 10 minutes

For each post, assign one status.

| Status | Criteria |
| --- | --- |
| Double down | At least 1.5x the 4-week median on reach or engagement, with useful comments or profile/follow signal. |
| Iterate | Near baseline but with one promising element, such as a strong hook, thread of comments, or useful objection. |
| Retire | Below 0.6x baseline twice, with weak qualitative signal. |
| Watch | Too early to judge or distorted by unusual distribution. |

### 3. Extract lessons, 15 minutes

Capture lessons in this structure so they can guide planning.

- **Hook.** What first line or framing earned attention?
- **Audience.** Which segment reacted, asked questions, or followed?
- **Promise.** What problem, outcome, or belief did the post center?
- **Proof.** Which examples, data points, stories, or screenshots made the idea credible?
- **Format.** Note text-only, carousel, list, story, contrarian point, teardown, or framework.
- **CTA.** Did the post invite the right action?
- **Distribution.** Did early engagement, tagging, or resharing change the result?

Each lesson should produce a decision, not just an observation. Write "turn the comparison post into a 3-part series" instead of "comparison posts performed well."

### 4. Feed the next planning cycle, 10 minutes

Update the content backlog during the review.

- Add `double-down`, `iterate`, `retire`, or `test-next` to each reviewed idea.
- Move winning topics into next week's candidate list.
- Convert strong comments and DMs into new post prompts.
- Pick one or two hypotheses for next week. Keep them testable, such as "pain-first hooks will increase comment quality on founder-audience posts."
- Reserve the posting mix before drafting starts:
  - 60% proven themes
  - 30% iterations on promising posts
  - 10% new experiments

### 5. Close with commitments, 5 minutes

End the review with a short handoff.

- What will be repeated next week?
- What will change in the next batch?
- What will be stopped?
- Which experiment needs a clean test?
- Who owns the updates to the content plan?

## Output artifact

Save one weekly review using the [weekly review template](./templates/weekly-analytics-review.md). The review should contain:

- Week reviewed
- Top posts and misses
- Metric baseline
- Lessons
- Backlog changes
- Next-week experiments
- Owner and date

## Decision rules

- Do not declare a winner before 72 hours unless the post has already passed the normal weekly range.
- Do not copy a winner blindly. Identify the element to repeat, such as the hook, story structure, topic, or CTA.
- Treat comments and DMs as source material. Preserve exact phrases when they reveal audience language.
- Review misses for diagnosis, not blame. A weak post can still produce a strong lesson.
- Keep experiments small. Test one meaningful variable at a time.
- If the same lesson appears for three weeks, turn it into a standing content rule.

## Weekly checklist

- [ ] Export or record post metrics.
- [ ] Calculate rolling 4-week medians.
- [ ] Label each post as double down, iterate, retire, or watch.
- [ ] Capture hook, audience, promise, proof, format, CTA, and distribution lessons.
- [ ] Update the backlog with labels and next actions.
- [ ] Select one or two experiments for next week.
- [ ] Save the review artifact.
- [ ] Start next-week planning from the updated backlog.
